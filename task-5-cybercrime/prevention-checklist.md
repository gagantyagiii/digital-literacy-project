# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students
# Financial & UPI Safety

 Remember that UPI PINs are only for sending money. Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money, cashbacks, or refunds. If an app asks for a PIN to "claim a reward," it is a scam.
 
Never use public Wi-Fi for financial transactions. Avoid making UPI payments or logging into bank accounts while connected to free campus, cafe, or transit Wi-Fi networks. Use your mobile data instead.

# Social Media & Communication

 Beware of urgent WhatsApp/Telegram job offers. Treat unsolicited messages offering high-paying part-time jobs, internships, or "like-and-earn" tasks with extreme skepticism. Legitimate companies do not recruit this way.

 Sanitize your digital footprint. Avoid sharing photos of your train tickets, university ID cards, or boarding passes online, as the barcodes contain sensitive personal data that can be exploited.

 Lock down your privacy settings. Regularly audit your social media accounts to ensure only trusted friends can see your posts, location data, and contact information.

# Device & Account Security

 Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA). Turn on 2FA for your email, university student portal, and all social media accounts to create an extra layer of defense against unauthorized access.

 Avoid pirated software and 'cracked' study materials. Do not download pirated software, games, or "free" certification exam dumps from untrusted forums, as these are primary vectors for ransomware and keyloggers.

 Verify educational portals before paying. Before paying fees for online courses or certifications, double-check the URL to ensure it is the official, secure domain (look for HTTPS and correct spelling) to avoid phishing clones.
