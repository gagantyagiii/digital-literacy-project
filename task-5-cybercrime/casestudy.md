# Case Study: Enterprise Ransomware Infection
# What it is: Ransomware is a highly disruptive type of malicious software that encrypts a victim's files or completely locks down their systems, demanding a financial payment (the ransom) in exchange for the decryption key.

# How it typically happens (Step-by-Step):

Initial Access: An employee unwittingly downloads a malicious payload, often through a deceptive spear-phishing email or by visiting a compromised website.

Execution: The malware installs itself silently in the background, establishing a connection with the attacker's command-and-control server.

Lateral Movement: Without strict internal network segmentation—a core routing and switching concept emphasized in CCNA training—the ransomware spreads rapidly from the initially infected workstation to critical connected servers and databases.

Encryption and Extortion: The software deploys strong encryption algorithms, rendering all critical data inaccessible. A ransom note is generated on the locked screens, typically demanding untraceable cryptocurrency.

Who is usually targeted: While anyone can be hit, cybercriminals primarily target organizations that cannot afford extended downtime—such as hospitals, local governments, and mid-sized enterprises. These entities often have valuable data but may lack the budget for enterprise-grade security teams.

Consequences: The impact extends far beyond the ransom itself. Victims face crippling operational paralysis, massive financial losses from halted productivity, and severe reputational damage. Recovering from such an attack usually forces an expensive, top-down overhaul of the organization's security posture to meet baseline industry standards, such as the strict access control and threat mitigation frameworks required for CompTIA Security+ compliance.
