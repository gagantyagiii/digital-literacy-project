# Do's for Responsible Social Media Use
Do audit your privacy settings. Regularly review who can see your posts and enable two-factor authentication to keep your accounts secure.

Do curate a positive digital footprint. Showcase your academic projects, skills, and positive achievements to build a strong professional presence.

Do assume everything is permanent. Remember that even posts on private accounts or disappearing stories can be screenshotted and shared by others.

Do network professionally. Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with peers, professors, and industry professionals in your target field.

Do take regular digital detoxes. Step away from your feeds to stay focused on your studies, offline relationships, and overall well-being.

# Don'ts for Responsible Social Media Use
Don't overshare personal information. Avoid posting real-time location data, your daily routines, or pictures of sensitive documents like your student ID.

Don't post compromising content. Avoid sharing anything, even as a joke, that you wouldn't want a future employer, scholarship committee, or university official to see.

Don't engage in toxic arguments. Avoid getting dragged into heated, emotional debates in comment sections that can quickly damage your digital reputation.

Don't vent about professors or peers. Complaining about your academic frustrations or specific individuals online can easily backfire and create disciplinary issues.

Don't let screen time hijack your schedule. Avoid letting doomscrolling eat into your study hours or disrupt your sleep schedule.
